Looking after yourself

school-refusal

Caring for young people experiencing school refusal can be physically and emotional challenging. They may not want the care you are providing. Or they may not be able to do the things you think will benefit them.

You may feel frustrated, exhausted, lonely or isolated. You may feel that your needs are not as important as your child's needs. You may not even feel entitled to seek or get help. But looking after someone else is not a reason to forget or neglect to look after yourself. If you’re not looking after your own wellbeing, you likely won’t be able to support someone else.

Aware of your own wellbeing.

Are you well?

Take a quick 2-minute wellbeing check-in quiz to understand what you’re experiencing and what you can do about it.

The quiz is suitable for people aged 18 and over looking to support themselves. Warwick-Edinburgh Mental Wellbeing Scale (see Medicare mental health quiz)

Build a support network

Connecting with other families with similar school refusal experiences may help you feel less stressed and alone. It may help you to feel more confident and connected in your parenting journey

ask for help script Sometimes when you are overwhelmed, you may not have the words to ask for help.

If you are a carer and you need emergency respite, call Carer Gateway on 1800 422 737.

If it's not an emergency, ReachOut Parents can help you:

  • access free and confidential one-on-one support with a professional parenting and family coach, via phone chat
