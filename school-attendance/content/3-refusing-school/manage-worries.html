How to manage worries

Help your child learn about anxiety and plan how to manage their worries.

Understand what anxiety is

Talk about worries

Plan how to manage worries

Think together about how to manage worries.

  • Give your child some positive statements to repeat to themselves in their own head, such as “I can do this” and “I went to school yesterday, and it was fine.”
  • Help them to remember other times when they did not want to do something but did it anyway and how they felt afterwards when it turned out okay.
  • Help them to understand that thoughts are just thoughts and can be unhelpful. Support them to think of a more helpful thought that could help them feel better such as "I can try my best", "I have managed before, I know I can do it again" or "I felt sick going yesterday but then I was fine by breaktime"
  • Explain that these feelings will pass. You can say to your child in a calm and comforting voice:
    • “I know that you think/feel ____. These feelings will pass once you are in school and get to class”.
    • “I know it’s hard to go back to school after the weekend/mid term/summer”
    • “It won’t always feel like this”
    • “You have PE today which you said you like”
    • “Don’t forget we have the footy game to look forward to this weekend”

Be a good role model

Be a good role model in managing anxiety

  • Model helpful ways of dealing with uncertainty by showing them how you accept what is outside your control and focus your effort on things you can control

  • Tell them about your own experiences. For example, what you found difficult at school or times when you felt worried, and how you coped without avoiding it.
