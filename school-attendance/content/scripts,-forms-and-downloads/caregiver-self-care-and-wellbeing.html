Caregiver self-care and wellbeing

Parenting a child who is experiencing school attendance challenges is a 24/7 job. Two parents talk about learning to manage their wellbeing and the importance of a support network.

  • How did parenting a child experiencing school attendance challenges impact on yourself and your family?
    • Chronicity
  • Can you describe any particular difficult experiences, how did that affect your wellbeing?
    • Crying and school bus (BD)
    • Camp (KG)
    • Feelings of failure, anxiety
  • How have you coped with the impacts?
    • What helped? (e.g. being calm, receiving empathetic messages from parent friends)
    • What didn't help?
  • What did you learn through that experience?
    • Eye-opening moments
    • Developing new coping strategies overtime
    • Parent can have separate identity from child. My child can't go but that doesn't mean I am failing. (BD)
    • Mental preparation as coping strategy (KG)
    • Changing catastrophic thinking
  • How do you cope with day-to-day experiences?
  • When did you come to realise that your child 'would be okay'?
  • What did you wish you had known as a parent?
    • Looking back, I couldn't see things getting better...
    • What would you say to your younger self?
  • What I wished others (parents, school) knew?
    • stigma of "being a bad parent"
  • What advice would you give to a caregiver to cope with the impacts of their child's school attendance challenge? - build a support network What do you think is the most important thing for parents to know and do to look after themselves
